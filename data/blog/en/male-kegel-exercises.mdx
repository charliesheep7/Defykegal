## What Most Men Get Wrong About Kegels

Most guys who try **male kegel exercises** make one of two mistakes: they squeeze the wrong muscles, or they squeeze the right ones and never let go. Both undo the whole point.

Kegels are resistance training for the pelvic floor — the sling of muscle running from your tailbone to your pubic bone. It supports the bladder, wraps the base of the penis, and fires during erection and climax. Done right, it is one of the few drug-free interventions with real clinical evidence behind firmer erections, better ejaculatory control, and fewer bladder leaks. Done wrong, it does nothing — or leaves you tense and sore. The technique below fixes both errors.

## Why Training the Pelvic Floor Works

Two of your pelvic floor muscles — the ischiocavernosus and bulbocavernosus — clamp down during an erection to trap blood in the erectile tissue. The same muscles contract rhythmically at climax to power ejaculation. Strengthen them and you are working the exact hardware behind firmness and control.

The evidence is solid. In a randomized controlled trial by physiotherapist Grace Dorey and colleagues, published in *BJU International*, 55 men with erectile dysfunction trained the pelvic floor for six months. **About 40 percent regained normal erectile function and another 35 percent improved**, versus little change in the control group ([full text via PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC1324914/); [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16104916/)). For a deeper look at that mechanism, see our guide to [pelvic floor muscles](/blog/pelvic-floor-muscles).

## How to Do Male Kegel Exercises

Form beats volume. Here is the Mayo Clinic-aligned method, step by step.

1. **Find the right muscles.** Imagine you're stopping the flow of urine midstream or holding in gas. The muscles that tighten are your pelvic floor. You should feel a lift and squeeze around the anus — not your abs, thighs, or glutes.
2. **Squeeze for 3 seconds, relax for 3.** Contract firmly, then release completely. Per [Cleveland Clinic](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/treatments/22211-kegel-exercises-for-men), the relaxation phase is as important as the squeeze — a floor that can't relax is tense, not strong.
3. **Do 10 to 15 reps, three times a day.** That's the standard clinical dose. Spread the three sets across your day rather than cramming them.
4. **Isolate the muscle.** Keep your stomach, thighs, and buttocks relaxed, and don't hold your breath. Breathe freely through every rep.
5. **Add quick flicks as you progress.** Once slow holds feel easy, layer in fast contract-release pulses. These build the reflexive control you actually use during sex.

Start lying down if you're new — it's the easiest position to isolate the muscle. Then progress to sitting and standing, where you'll use the muscle in real life. For a full structured routine, see our [male pelvic floor workout](/blog/male-pelvic-floor-workout).

## Building the Habit

Here's the honest part: the technique takes five minutes to learn and months to pay off. The men who see results aren't the ones who do 100 reps in a burst — they're the ones who never miss a day. [Healthline](https://www.healthline.com/health/kegel-exercises-for-men) notes results typically show up within a few weeks to a few months of regular practice, with bladder control often improving first.

The trick is to anchor kegels to something you already do daily. Do a set while brushing your teeth, one at a red light, one before you fall asleep. Three sets, three moments — no extra time carved out of your day.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Never fully relaxing.** The single biggest error. Squeezing all day without release leaves the pelvic floor chronically tight — a condition that can actually worsen pain and control. Train both directions.
- **Recruiting the wrong muscles.** If your abs, glutes, or thighs tense up, you're cheating. Put a hand on your stomach; it should stay soft.
- **Holding your breath.** Bearing down raises abdominal pressure and pushes against the muscle you're trying to lift. Breathe normally.
- **Doing them only in one position.** Real-world control means training standing and moving, not just lying on the couch.

## Common Questions

**Can I do male kegel exercises anywhere?**
Yes. Once you can isolate the muscle, they're completely invisible. Desk, car, checkout line — no one can tell.

**Will kegels make my erections firmer?**
The pelvic floor traps blood in the penis, so strengthening it supports firmer erections — the mechanism behind the Dorey trial results. If ED is the goal, pair this with our overview of [erectile dysfunction treatment](/blog/erectile-dysfunction-treatment).

**Do kegels help premature ejaculation?**
Research says yes. Pelvic floor training has been shown to significantly lengthen ejaculatory control time. Combine kegels with the [stop-start method](/blog/stop-start-method-for-premature-ejaculation) for the strongest effect.

**What if I'm not sure I'm doing them right?**
A pelvic floor physiotherapist can confirm your form with biofeedback. You can also reach out through the [Defy support page](/support) with questions.

## The Bottom Line

Male kegel exercises train a real muscle group with real jobs — erections, ejaculatory control, and bladder function — and the clinical record backs meaningful improvement across all three. The method is simple: find the muscle, squeeze and fully relax, ten to fifteen reps, three times a day.

The hard part is showing up every day. Do that for a couple of months and the payoff compounds.
