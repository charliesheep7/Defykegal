Search "sex pills for men" and you get two completely different products wearing the same label. One is a class of prescription drugs with decades of trial data behind it. The other is a shelf of capsules with names in all caps and a number like 700000 on the box, sold with no oversight and, according to the FDA's own testing, often spiked with the first category's active ingredients.

Knowing which is which matters more than any brand comparison. Here is what the evidence actually shows about both, and the one non-pill approach with randomized-trial support behind it.

## What Prescription ED Pills Do

Sildenafil, tadalafil and vardenafil are PDE5 inhibitors. They do not create arousal or desire. They block an enzyme that breaks down the signalling molecule responsible for relaxing the smooth muscle in penile arteries, which means **more blood flows in when you are already aroused**. No arousal, no effect. That surprises a lot of men expecting a switch.

The efficacy numbers are solid. A [comparative meta-analysis in the International Journal of Impotence Research](https://www.nature.com/articles/3901395) pooled fixed-dose randomized trials and found successful intercourse rates of **65 percent for sildenafil, 62 percent for tadalafil and 59 percent for vardenafil**, against placebo rates of 23 to 28 percent. Improved-erection rates ran higher, at 76, 75 and 71 percent respectively.

Read those numbers honestly in both directions. They are genuinely effective, and roughly a third of men still do not get the result they wanted. Side effects are common but usually mild, with headache reported in 13 to 17 percent of users. The [NHS guidance on erection problems](https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/erection-problems-erectile-dysfunction/) covers the prescribing picture, including why these drugs are off-limits if you take nitrates.

## The Over-the-Counter Problem

Now the other shelf. Male enhancement supplements are sold as food products, which means they reach the market without proving either safety or effectiveness first.

The consequences are documented rather than theoretical. The FDA maintains a running list of sexual-enhancement products found to contain hidden drug ingredients, and as [Operation Supplement Safety summarises](https://www.opss.org/article/sexual-enhancement-products-and-hidden-ingredients), sexual enhancement is **the single largest category of adulterated supplements**. Hundreds of products have been flagged. The most common hidden ingredients are the exact PDE5 inhibitors sold on prescription, alongside surprises like diclofenac, an anti-inflammatory, and in some cases diabetes medication.

[Harvard Health](https://content.health.harvard.edu/blog/warning-hidden-ingredients-in-supplements-for-weight-loss-and-male-enhancement) puts the risk plainly: an unlabelled dose of sildenafil in a capsule you bought at a gas station can interact with nitrate heart medication and drop your blood pressure to dangerous levels. You do not know the dose. You do not know the interactions. You do not know what else is in there.

Which produces the uncomfortable conclusion: **the OTC pill that seems to work is often the one that has been contaminated.** The genuinely herbal ones, the ones with no hidden pharmaceuticals, generally have thin evidence and produce little. ## Why Men Reach for Pills First

A pill is a clean transaction. Take it, wait, get a result, no conversation with anyone. Compare that with booking a urology appointment to discuss erections, and the appeal is obvious.

But that shortcut skips the diagnostic step, and that step matters. Erectile difficulty is frequently the first visible sign of cardiovascular disease, low testosterone, poorly controlled diabetes, or a medication side effect. A pill masks the symptom while the underlying cause continues. A doctor who finds the cause changes the trajectory.

The shortcut also skips the one intervention that treats a mechanical contributor rather than the symptom.

## The Non-Pill Option With Trial Data

Erections are not just an inflow problem. Getting blood in is half of it. **Keeping it there is the other half, and that job belongs to muscle.**

The bulbocavernosus and ischiocavernosus muscles sit in your pelvic floor and wrap around the base of the penis. When you are erect they contract to compress the veins that would otherwise drain the shaft, maintaining internal pressure. Weak or slow-to-fire muscles let pressure leak. That is the erection that starts strong and fades, or never reaches full rigidity even with good blood flow.

The trial evidence is real. [Dorey and colleagues (2004)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15527607/), a randomized controlled trial published in the British Journal of General Practice, tested pelvic floor muscle exercises as a treatment for erectile dysfunction and found **about 40 percent of men regained normal erectile function, with roughly another third improving meaningfully**. Set that against the placebo rates above and it is a serious result for an intervention with no side effects and no cost.

It is also slower. Pills work in an hour. Training takes weeks. That trade-off is the honest pitch — and the two are not mutually exclusive, since they address different halves of the same mechanism.

## How to Approach This Sensibly

1. **Get a diagnosis before a prescription.** Ask your doctor about cardiovascular risk, testosterone, blood sugar, and any medication you already take. ED is a symptom, and the cause is worth knowing.
2. **If you use PDE5 inhibitors, use prescribed ones.** Same molecule, known dose, real oversight, and a doctor who has checked your nitrate status.
3. **Skip the OTC enhancement aisle.** Check any product you already own against the FDA's notification list before taking another one.
4. **Start pelvic floor training in parallel.** It takes a few minutes a day and addresses the part no pill touches. Our [kegels for men](/blog/kegels-for-men) guide covers finding the right muscle and avoiding the common form errors.
5. **Fix the boring inputs.** Sleep, alcohol intake, smoking, and cardiovascular fitness all move erectile function measurably. They are unglamorous and they work.

## What Progress Looks Like

With pelvic floor training, expect the first two weeks to be about control rather than results — learning to contract the right muscle without recruiting your glutes or abs. Most men report firmer morning erections somewhere in weeks three to six. Dorey's protocol ran three months, which is where the trial measured its main outcomes.

If nothing has shifted after three consistent months, that is useful information, not failure. It points toward a vascular or hormonal cause worth investigating properly. ## The Bottom Line

Prescription ED pills work for roughly two out of three men and come with real oversight. Over-the-counter sex pills for men are a category the FDA has flagged hundreds of times for hidden pharmaceuticals, which means the effective ones are effective for a reason you did not consent to.

The third option gets ignored because it asks for consistency instead of a credit card. Pelvic floor training has randomized-trial evidence, no side effects, and it targets the mechanism a pill cannot reach.
