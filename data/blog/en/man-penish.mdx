Most men know surprisingly little about how their own body actually works down there — which is understandable, since nobody teaches it. But a clear picture of how the penis is built, how erections happen, and what counts as normal takes a lot of anxiety off the table and points you toward the things that genuinely improve performance.

Here is the straightforward guide: the anatomy, the mechanics of an erection, the real numbers on size, and the simple habits that keep a man's penis healthy.

## How the Penis Is Built

The penis is simpler than it seems, and understanding its parts explains everything about how it works. According to the [Cleveland Clinic's anatomy overview](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/body/penis), the key structures are:

- **The corpora cavernosa.** Two parallel chambers running the length of the shaft, filled with a sponge-like maze of blood vessels. These are the engine of an erection — when they fill with blood, the penis becomes firm.
- **The corpus spongiosum.** A third chamber on the underside that surrounds the urethra and keeps it open during an erection.
- **The urethra.** The tube that carries both urine and semen out through the tip.
- **The glans.** The head at the end of the shaft, rich in nerve endings.
- **The meatus.** The opening at the tip where urine and semen exit.

Wrapped around and beneath all of this at the base sit the pelvic floor muscles — and those turn out to be more important to performance than most men realize.

## How an Erection Actually Works

An erection is a plumbing event driven by nerves and muscles. Here is the sequence, drawn from the [Cleveland Clinic's explanation of erections](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/articles/10036-erection):

1. **Arousal starts in the brain.** Physical touch or mental stimulation triggers nerve signals that travel to the penis.
2. **Blood rushes in.** Those signals relax the smooth muscle inside the corpora cavernosa, opening the spongy spaces so blood floods in and the penis swells and stiffens.
3. **The pelvic floor traps it.** The muscles at the base — part of your [pelvic floor](/blog/pelvic-floor-muscles) — clamp down on the veins that drain the penis, trapping blood under pressure. This is what turns a swelling into a rigid erection.
4. **Firmness holds.** As long as arousal and that muscular clamp continue, the erection stays firm.

That third step is the one men overlook. If the pelvic floor muscles are weak, blood leaks back out and erections soften — which is exactly why training them improves firmness. Our guide to how [kegels work for men](/blog/kegels-for-men) breaks down the mechanism.

## What Counts as Normal Size

Size anxiety is common and mostly misplaced. The best data comes from a 2015 systematic review in *BJU International* that pooled measurements from up to 15,521 men. It found an average erect length of **13.12 cm (5.16 inches)** and an average erect circumference of **11.66 cm (4.59 inches)**. The [study built size nomograms](https://www.medicalnewstoday.com/articles/290226) showing that the vast majority of men fall within a fairly narrow band around those averages.

A few things worth knowing:

- **Flaccid size predicts erect size poorly.** So-called "growers" gain far more from flaccid to erect than "showers," which is completely normal variation.
- **A slight curve is normal.** A new, painful, or worsening curve can signal Peyronie's disease and deserves a checkup — see our overview of a [curved penis](/blog/curved-penis) for context.
- **Growth stops after puberty.** If you are wondering when development finishes, our guide on [when the penis stops growing](/blog/when-does-your-penis-stop-growing) covers the timeline.

## Keeping a Man's Penis Healthy

Penile health is mostly cardiovascular health, because the whole system runs on blood flow. The habits that protect your heart protect your erections:

- **Move regularly.** Cardiovascular exercise keeps the blood vessels that feed the penis in good shape.
- **Eat and sleep well.** A healthy diet, good sleep, and a healthy weight all support blood flow and testosterone.
- **Do not smoke.** Smoking damages the small blood vessels erections depend on — one of the most direct ways to harm performance.
- **Train your pelvic floor.** This adds firmness the cardiovascular basics alone cannot, by strengthening the muscular clamp that holds an erection.
- **Pay attention to changes.** New pain, lumps, sores, or a sudden curve deserve a doctor's visit rather than a wait-and-see.

## Closing

The penis is a straightforward piece of engineering: spongy chambers that fill with blood, a urethra for output, and pelvic floor muscles that trap the blood to create firmness. Most men are more normal than they fear, and the biggest lever on performance is not size but blood flow and pelvic floor strength — both of which you can improve at any age.
